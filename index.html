Who is Peter Kefalas?

Meet the Republican Candidate Running for NYC Comptroller With Little Funding & Low Recognition

Megan Mandrachio | October 13th, 2025

Peter Kefalas is running as the Republican candidate for New York City Comptroller in the 2025 general election. As of the latest available data from to the New York City Campaign Finance Board, His campaign for has reported a total of $807 in monetary contributions. This funding level places his campaign among the lower-funded candidates in the race.

Despite limited financial resources, Kefalas has maintained an active presence through grassroots organizing, media engagement, and direct outreach to voters. His campaign emphasizes fiscal accountability, transparency, and oversight of city finances.

The following sections outline his background, priorities as Comptroller, and involvement in local civic and economic matters.

Background

Peter Kefalas is a lifelong resident of Queens, New York and Republican candidate runnng for New York City Comptroller in the 2025 general election. Prior to running for office, he worked for a news and media outlet (QNS) that covers public service and political issues affecting the lives of New Yorkers.

“I am running for NYC Comptroller because our city is at a crossroads. Reckless spending, financial waste, and lack of accountability have led to record-high taxes, deteriorating public safety, and an affordability crisis. As your Comptroller, I will fight for taxpayers, conduct real oversight, and ensure your money is spent wisely. New York City deserves a fiscal watchdog who puts accountability, transparency, and responsibility first.”
— Peter Kefalas, as featured on NYC Votes

Priorities as Comptroller

If elected, Peter Kefalas aims to bring fiscal accountability and transparency to city government. His stated goals focus on improving the management of city resources and ensuring that public funds are used efficiently.

Top 3 issues:

  • Stop financial waste and fraud.
  • Strengthen public safety funding.
  • Fight inflation and lower taxes.

He plans to use independent audits, budgeting, and oversight to ensure taxpayer dollars are spent responsibly.

Local Economy

According to his campaign website, Peter Kefalas has worked as a small business owner and consultant, helping companies grow and training sales teams across multiple industries. His experience in financial oversight and operational efficiency is now applied to advocating for transparency and fiscal discipline in government. Source

Civic Engagement

Peter Kefalas has been active in his community and in local civic matters. In 2024, he participated in efforts in Rego Park to ensure community input in the planning of new homeless shelters. Through organizing residents and engaging with local media, he highlighted the importance of transparency in city planning decisions.

Kefalas has also been involved with QNS Voice, a local media outlet that covers public service and political issues affecting New Yorkers. Through reporting and interviews, the outlet provides coverage of city government and civic matters that may not appear in mainstream media. In addition, he authored 1984 Bureaucratic Tentacles: Rise and Fall of The Chevron Doctrine, published in August 2024, which examines changes in federal administrative law. His work in both media and writing reflects an interest in transparency and government accountability at multiple levels.
